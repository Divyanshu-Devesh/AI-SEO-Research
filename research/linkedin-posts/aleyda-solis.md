# Topic:
A 3-layer framework for measuring AI Search Business Impact Metrics.

# Summary:
The framework establishes a rigorous, tiered methodology for measuring the business impact of AI Search (e.g., Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google Overviews, Microsoft Copilot). Rather than reporting a single, potentially misleading "AI Impact" figure, the framework breaks metrics into four distinct confidence layers: Observed, Own Proxy, Third-Party Proxy, and Modelled. This structured taxonomy allows digital marketers and data analysts to clearly distinguish between direct, hard-coded attribution, inferred first-party signals, competitive external benchmarks, and speculative planning estimates.

# Key Takeaways:
- Observed (High Confidence – Direct Attribution)
  > AI-Referred Sessions: Measured directly via GA4 referral traffic. This represents the absolute "floor" of your AI traffic, not the ceiling.

  > AI Conversion Rate & Revenue per Visit: Used to evaluate the actual intent quality of AI traffic against traditional organic search benchmarks.

  > AI-Assisted Conversions: Calculated via multi-touch attribution and path exploration to capture conversions where an AI platform was an introductory or middle-touch point rather than the final click

- Own Proxy (Medium-High Confidence – First-Party Inferences)
  > Branded/Direct/Surfaced-Page Demand: Tracking traffic lifts on specific landing pages that are heavily cited by AI engines to detect "dark" or un-attributable AI influence.

  > Survey AI Discovery Rate: Explicitly asking users via post-purchase or signup surveys: "Did you first hear about us via an AI assistant?"

  > BWT AI Performance: Utilizing Bing Webmaster Tools data to track actual citation visibility, cited pages, and grounding queries across Microsoft's AI ecosystem.

- Third-Party Proxy (Medium-Low Confidence – Competitive Benchmarking)
  > Traffic Share vs. Peers: Using external tools to estimate your share of AI-referred voice compared to direct competitors.

  > Prompt Samples per Top Landing Page: Sampling the specific questions and user prompts that trigger your links to map out user intent gaps.

  > Platform Mix vs. Peer Average: Identifying platform dependencies (e.g., over-indexing on ChatGPT but under-performing on Perplexity) to highlight fragility risks.

- Modelled (Low Confidence – Macro Planning)
  > Modelled Influenced Pipeline/Revenue: Speculative projections calculated using historical conversion rates applied against incremental branded demand over a baseline. It is strictly a budget/planning tool, never to be communicated as hard-attributed proof.


# Why Relevant:
1. Solving the "Dark AI" Attribution Crisis
AI search engines do not behave like traditional search engines. They frequently synthesize information without a click, serve as un-trackable conversational touchpoints, or strip out clean referral data. Relying purely on traditional Google Analytics tracking means missing the vast majority of AI's actual business impact. This framework gives marketers an operational playbook to track hidden downstream demand without resorting to deceptive or exaggerated numbers.

2. Preserving Executive Trust
Data teams frequently lose credibility with the C-suite by over-promising and over-claiming revenue from new channels. By cleanly categorizing metrics into confidence tiers, an organization can present hard facts to the CFO while simultaneously using proxy and modeled data to guide the CMO's long-term AI optimization strategies.
