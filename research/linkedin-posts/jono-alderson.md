# Topic:
Technical Debt becoming an AI problem.

# Summary:
Jono Alderson argues that the frantic business debate over which AI protocols (like llms.txt or WebMCP) to invest in misses the point entirely. The tech industry treats AI as a passing storm that will eventually clear into a stable landscape, but the reality is an era of permanently accelerating change. The true barrier to AI adoption isn't the complexity of the new protocols themselves, but the crushing weight of internal technical debt (fragmented CMSs, siloed data, poor governance). The organizations succeeding today aren't necessarily "AI-first"; they are simply the ones that spent years doing the unglamorous work of reducing internal complexity, making adaptation cheap, fast, and risk-free.

# Key Takeaways:
- Technical debt isn't just a IT maintenance line-item; it is a direct tax on agility. It is defined by how expensive future change has become for your organization.
- Trying to guess which tech standard will "win" is a losing strategy because the intervals between disruptions are shrinking. The ability to respond quickly is far more valuable than the ability to choose correctly.
- Companies struggle to expose their data to external AI models because they haven’t even figured out how to expose that data clearly to themselves.
- Reduced complexity creates a massive operational divide. Clean organizations can deploy a new standard in six hours as an "experiment" to see what happens. Debt-ridden organizations require a six-month "project" filled with budgeting, politics, and risk assessments.
- The objective shouldn't be masterfully picking the right AI standards. The goal is to build an organizational architecture so lean and modular that you no longer need to care which standard wins.

# Why Relevant:
For the past few years, the corporate world has been obsessed with building proprietary AI models or flashy front-end tools. This article flips the script, highlighting that discoverability and integration are the next battlegrounds. If LLMs and AI agents cannot easily crawl, understand, or ingest your company's data because it's locked in six different legacy content management systems, your business effectively becomes invisible to the modern AI ecosystem.
